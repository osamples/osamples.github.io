Olivia Sanders

With a commitment to be continuously curious and a relentless learner, I serve as a Data Scientist for the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children (NCMEC) and have earned a M.S. in Data Science degree from Lipscomb University. Coupled with my Mathematics degree from Samford University, it is clear that I have a passion for analytical processes and problem solving. This ability to think logically and symbolically about quantitative, spatial, and abstract relationships was applied to my responsibilities as a Data Analyst, Cloud Security Engineer, and Lead Cloud Security Data Scientist for IHS Markit where I applied my technical and detail-oriented focus to improving the products for our clients and develop new tools such as graph technologies that made security more clear for InfoSec as a whole.

Having a keen awareness of quantitative information in the world around me has inspired a strong curiosity and desire to attain knowledge. Through multiple study abroad programs, I was able to apply this curiosity and desire. In fact, through my trips to Spain, Israel, Costa Rica, and Panama, I learned to creatively solve problems by applying familiar concepts to unfamiliar situations, and was able to be flexible and adaptable to rapidly changing situations and new environments.

While I have a technical background, I also seek the knowledge of community and culture. I earned a double minor in Spanish and Latin American Studies which has been a source of mind opening material and experiences, developing strong interpersonal skills within me. I have earned leadership and volunteer opportunities such as a member of IHS Markit's Graduate program because of these interpersonal skills.

These experiences have stimulated my professional drive. With a strong desire to continue to apply my quantitative knowledge and interpersonal skills in the work place, I prioritize these aspects through my role as a Data Scientist.

Skills: Python, R, AWS technologies, SQL, Java
